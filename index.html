👨‍🔬 About Julius Sumner Miller

Julius Sumner Miller (1909–1987) was an American-born physicist and science educator who became one of the most beloved science communicators in the world. Born in Stoughton, Massachusetts, Miller's infectious enthusiasm for physics captivated audiences across the United States and Australia.

His television series "Why Is It So?" — produced by the Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC) — made him a household name. With theatrical flair, dramatic gestures, and genuine wonder, Miller demonstrated that physics is not just a subject but a way of seeing the world.

He taught at El Camino College in California and held positions at several universities, but his greatest classroom was the television studio, where millions watched him challenge them with his signature catchphrase: "Why is it so?"

Key fact: Miller once performed his physics demonstrations for Albert Einstein and was praised for his ability to make complex physics tangible and exciting.
